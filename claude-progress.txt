### Round 17: A/B Experiment Results (2026-03-13) — COMPLETE

#### v1-strict vs v2-relaxed on bmark-cli-002 baseline

| Experiment | Loops | Keeps | KeepRate | L→Tgt | T→Tgt(s) | Score Range |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| bmark-cli-002-v1 | 9 | 9 | 1.0000 | 9 | 2907 | 0.2500→1.0000 |
| bmark-cli-002-v2 | 5 | 0 | 0.0000 | ∞ | ∞ | 0.2500→0.2500 |

#### v2-relaxed Root Cause: PROGRESS.md Rollback Loop
1. CODE_AUDIT fails (files_changed: 6→10→10→9→7, cap=5) → git reset --hard
2. PROGRESS.md reverts to scaffold state (Loop: 0)
3. Agent reads rolled-back PROGRESS.md → sees no progress → tries full implementation again
4. Hypothesis text unchanged across all 5 loops (always scaffold initial text)

#### Key Finding
`max_files_changed` is the dominant behavioral parameter.
Tight caps (3) enforce 1-feature-per-loop. Relaxed caps (5) invite scope creep.
The Arena Contract alone cannot enforce discipline — the cap IS the mechanism.

#### Proposed v3
- Same tight caps as v1 (files=3/2, diff=150)
- Arena Contract addition: "Read EVALS/ledger.jsonl before planning" (survives rollback)
- Agent can learn from discard_audit without relying on PROGRESS.md

#### Artifact
`logs/ab-observation-v1v2-001.md` — full per-loop breakdown and root cause analysis
